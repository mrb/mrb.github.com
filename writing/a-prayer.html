A Prayer for Peace in Palestine

The closest I have ever come to living in a war zone was waking up across the river from the World Trade Center in Queens on September 11th, 2001 at around 11AM.

*  *  *

I open my curtain and the sky is bright blue. Very few clouds. I stretch and sit in front of my computer. Turn on the screen. It’s covered with Instant Messages.

“Are you okay?"
"Where are you?"
"Dude???”
"Bro grab your AK, we’re at war!"
“Call me"

I looked around for my small blue Nokia cell phone. Sweatily I grab it.

Voicemails but no reception. No way to hear them.

My heart is a distraction, I want to talk to someone.

No land line and the internet is slow.

All I smell is cigarettes and stale beer. I open a window.

My roommate is at work.

I turn on the television.

*  *  *

What does it mean to be from a place?

What does the world owe you, and what do you owe the world, because of the coordinates of your emergence from the womb?

Were you born from a person or born from the earth?

*  *  *

I was born and learned that we share a God. I was born and learned that we share a God and share blood and are a family. I was born and learned that we share a God and share blood and are a family and some people are not in this family. I was born and learned that we share a God and share blood and are a family and some people are not in this family and we must keep them away from our own.
